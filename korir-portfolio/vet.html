Why Wellness Exams Matter

Veterinary wellness examinations are comprehensive health assessments designed to maintain your animal's well-being through preventive care. These regular check-ups allow veterinarians to establish baseline health metrics, detect potential issues before they become serious, and provide personalized care recommendations based on your animal's species, age, and lifestyle.

Vaccinations & Immunization Plans

Keep your furry family members healthy with our comprehensive vaccination programs

How Vaccines Work

Vaccines contain modified or killed viruses/bacteria that stimulate your pet's immune system to produce protective antibodies without causing the actual disease. This prepares their immune system to fight off future infections if they're exposed to the real pathogen.

Benefits of Vaccination

  • Prevents serious, potentially fatal diseases
  • Reduces spread of contagious illnesses
  • More cost-effective than treating diseases
  • Required for boarding, grooming, and travel
  • Protects human family members from zoonotic diseases

Core Vaccines

Recommended for all pets regardless of lifestyle

  • Rabies
  • Canine/Feline Distemper
  • Parvovirus (dogs)
  • Calicivirus (cats)
  • Herpesvirus (cats)

Non-Core Vaccines

Recommended based on individual risk factors

  • Bordetella (Kennel Cough)
  • Leptospirosis
  • Lyme Disease
  • Feline Leukemia
  • Canine Influenza

Vaccine Safety & What to Expect

All vaccines used at our clinic undergo rigorous testing and meet the highest safety standards. While adverse reactions are rare, we monitor all pets after vaccination.

Mild reactions may include:

  • Mild discomfort at injection site
  • Lethargy for 24-48 hours
  • Mild fever
  • Reduced appetite

During Your Visit:

  • Comprehensive health examination before vaccination
  • Discussion of your pet's lifestyle and risk factors
  • Gentle handling techniques to reduce stress
  • Quick, virtually painless injections
  • Post-vaccination observation period

Frequently Asked Questions

Are vaccines really necessary for indoor pets?

Yes, even indoor pets can be exposed to diseases. Viruses can be brought into your home on shoes, clothing, or through open windows. Rabies vaccination is also legally required in most areas.

Can my pet have a reaction to vaccines?

Mild reactions like soreness at the injection site or slight lethargy can occur but typically resolve within 24-48 hours. Serious reactions are rare. We monitor pets after vaccination.

Why does my pet need booster shots?

Booster shots are necessary to maintain your pet's immunity. Initial vaccines provide protection, but immunity can wane over time. Boosters "remind" the immune system to continue producing protective antibodies.

Can older pets stop getting vaccinations?

Senior pets may have different vaccination needs based on their health status, but core vaccines are still important. We tailor vaccination plans for senior pets, considering their specific health conditions.
